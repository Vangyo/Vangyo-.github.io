#Distribution of Temperature
plt.figure(figsize=(12,6))
sns.histplot(data=df, x='Temperature', bins=56, kde=True)
plt.title('Distribution of Temperature')
plt.xlabel('Temperature')
plt.ylabel('Count')
plt.show()

The first chart, "Distribution of Temperature," shows the relationship between temperature and bike rentals. From the graph, it's clear that the highest number of rentals occurs when the temperature is between 18 to 27 degrees Celsius. These temperatures are most common during spring and fall in San Francisco, which explains why those seasons see the highest ridership. In contrast, on extremely hot or cold days, the number of rentals drops to about one-eighth of what it is during peak periods. This suggests that if the company can find ways to increase usage during off-peak weather, it has a strong opportunity to boost overall profits.

Warm colors like red represent strong positive correlation, which means those conditions have a higher percentage to influence the rentals. Cooler colors like blue have lower percentage to impact the rentals. Based on the dataset, temperature has the strongest positive correlation with rentals, which means people prefer to rent bikes on warm and comfortable days. On the other hand, humidity and rainfall have negative correlation, showing that bad weather conditions can reduce demand. Other factors like visibility and dew point temperature also show a weak but positive relationship, which means that clear and comfortable conditions, for example, sunny days or temperatures between 22 to 26 degree Celsius may make more people want to use the bike.

Questions

1. In your analytical opinion, why are the majority of users renting bicycles?

Most people seem to be renting bikes not just for fun, but mainly for practical reasons like commuting or getting around the city quickly. The data shows a spike in rentals during weekday rush hours especially in the mornings and late afternoons which lines up perfectly with typical work schedules. On top of that, a large number of users are subscribers rather than one time riders, suggesting that it's mostly locals, not tourists, who rely on the service. In a busy city like San Francisco, where parking is a hassle and traffic is often a nightmare, bikes offer a faster and more convenient alternative. Plus, the low cost and eco friendly nature of bike sharing make it an attractive option for city dwellers who care about saving money and reducing their environmental impact.

2. Are there ideal weather conditions that create high bike rental demand? If so, what are they?

Yes, bike rentals clearly go up when the weather is nice. People are more likely to ride when the temperature is comfortable typically between 18 to 24 degrees Celsius. Sunny or partly cloudy days tend to bring out more riders, while rainy days or extreme heat or cold usually keep people away. Wind and humidity also play a role calm, dry days are definitely more appealing. All of this helps explain why spring and fall are the busiest seasons for bike rentals, while winter tends to be much slower because of the less favorable weather.

3. How should the company strategize based on your results? In other words, what actionable steps can the company take in order to take advantage of your insights.

The company could run seasonal promotions targeting annual subscribers in the spring and fall to boost ridership during these high demand periods. On days with poor weather or during slower seasons, offering discounts or loyalty points could help encourage continued use. A point system that lets Bay Wheels users earn rewards toward Lyft rides, for example, could be a smart way to attract new customers and increase usage frequency. It would also be beneficial to implement a system that uses real time weather data to predict demand and optimize bike distribution accordingly. Lastly, adding features to the app such as route suggestions based on weather and traffic, or tracking calories burned and CO₂ saved would enhance the user experience and help turn occasional riders into loyal ones.

Business Insights

1.Bike rentals tend to peak during the morning and evening rush hours, suggesting that most users are local commuters rather than tourists. This shows that Bay Wheels is being used more as a practical daily transportation option than for leisure. To take advantage of this trend, the company could increase bike availability during commute hours and introduce commuter focused perks like "commuter passes" or special bundles. These steps would likely boost customer satisfaction and encourage long term use.

2.Bike rentals increase significantly on clear and mild days, typically when temperatures range between 15 to 27 degrees Celsius. On the other hand, usage tends to drop on rainy days or when the weather is extremely hot or cold. Given this seasonal fluctuation, it's important for the company to adjust its pricing and marketing strategies based on demand. For example, boosting bike availability and running promotions in the spring and fall, while offering discounts or reward points during winter or rainy days, could help maintain steady revenue throughout the year. Additionally, installing windshields on bike handlebars similar to those used on motorcycles might encourage more people to ride during colder months.

3.By using real time weather forecasts and demand predictions, Bay Wheels can optimize bike distribution and inventory management, leading to greater operational efficiency. In addition, adding features to the app such as suggested routes based on weather and traffic conditions, visual displays of calories burned and CO₂ emissions reduced, and integration with Lyft’s point system can significantly enhance the user experience. These features are likely to appeal to health and eco conscious users, encouraging repeat rides and helping attract new customers through word of mouth.

Conclusion

This analysis makes it clear that Bay Wheels is used more by local residents for commuting and everyday travel than by tourists. Usage peaks during the morning and evening rush hours, showing that bike rentals have become a part of people’s daily routines. We also found that rental numbers increase significantly on days with comfortable temperatures around 18 to 24 degrees Celsius which are typical of San Francisco’s spring and fall. These seasons clearly drive the highest demand. On the other hand, bike usage tends to drop sharply on rainy days or when the weather is too hot or too cold. Given these trends, Bay Wheels has a strong opportunity to expand its user base by reinforcing service availability during commuting hours and high-demand seasons, while offering discounts or reward points during bad weather and off-peak times. Implementing real-time weather-based inventory adjustments, and enhancing the app with features like suggested routes, calorie and CO₂ tracking, and integration with Lyft’s reward system, could also make the service more appealing. By continuing to build a flexible, user-centered service, Bay Wheels has the potential to grow as an essential part of urban life and earn even greater support from the community.
